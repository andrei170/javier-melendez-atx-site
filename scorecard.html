A solid site, competing on the wrong thing.

This is not a rescue job. You already have more than most Austin agents at your stage: a real multi-page site, a home-valuation tool, live IDX search, and authentic first-time-buyer reviews. The gap is not effort, it is positioning and capture. The site leads with "Austin Real Estate Expert" (the one phrase 17,000-plus agents share) and buries the advantage no competitor can copy: a bilingual, Austin-raised agent who runs the whole deal in Spanish. Sharpen that, add a real capture layer, and the same assets become a lead machine.

Fixing the failing areas - positioning, the bilingual funnel, lead capture, surfaced proof, and tracking - keeps everything that already works and turns a well-built brochure into a measurable, bilingual lead engine.

Priority Roadmap · Fix First
  • Re-lead the homepage with the bilingual, Austin-raised story and a named first-time-buyer program
  • Deploy the free "Austin First-Time Buyer's Roadmap" magnet with name + email capture (EN & ES)
  • Stand up branded email (javier@workwithjavi.com) and route all capture through it
  • Install tracking: GA4, Search Console, and a conversion / ads pixel
Build Next · Compounding Growth
  • Full Spanish-language funnel + nurture sequence, with segmented EN / ES lists
  • Per-suburb landing pages (Round Rock, Georgetown, Leander, Cedar Park, Pflugerville and more)
  • Surface real Google / Zillow reviews and a proof spine (families served, rating) once confirmed
  • Reactivate the vlog on a repeatable cadence and repurpose clips to Instagram and YouTube

The bilingual lane is open now.

No dominant Austin personal-brand agent has claimed the full-transaction bilingual, first-time-buyer position for the metro's roughly one-third Hispanic market. You already own the advantage and paid for the foundation. The move is to point them at each other - and be the first to do it.
