Executive Summary

Javier already has more online than most Austin agents at his stage: a real multi-page site (workwithjavi.com), a home-valuation tool, IDX search, a YouTube vlog, and profiles on every platform. The foundation is real and worth defending.

The problem is positioning, not presence. His un-copyable edge - a bilingual, Austin-raised agent who runs the entire transaction (contracts to closing) in Spanish for the metro's ~one-third Hispanic population - is nowhere near the front of his brand.

  • Wrong headline. "Austin Real Estate Expert" is a phrase all ~17,000-plus metro agents can claim - the most crowded search term in the market.
  • Buried advantage. He competes on the one word he cannot win, and hides the one edge no competitor can copy.

This is not a "you are invisible" audit. It is a "your own website is burying a bilingual, Austin-native advantage" audit. The fixes are surgical:

  • Lead with the bilingual, Austin-raised story.
  • Turn "good with first-timers" into a named, step-by-step program.
  • Build a visible proof spine and a lead-capture funnel so not-ready browsers get followed up, not lost.

What this means in plain terms

  1. A one-third-Hispanic market is under-served at the transaction level. Many first-time and investor buyers want an agent who runs the whole deal in Spanish - contracts, negotiation, lender calls, closing. Most agents hand a Spanish-speaking family to a translator mid-purchase. Javier does the opposite: a structurally rare, high-trust position, currently invisible on his site.
  2. The buyer's journey starts online but ends with an agent. 100% of buyers use the internet, ~44-47% start with online search, yet ~88% still close through an agent. The site's job is not to replace Javier - it is to win the online-first stage and convert attention into a booked call. Today it attracts but does not capture.
  3. ~17,000-plus agents compete on the same generic phrase. "Austin real estate expert" is undifferentiated noise. Buyers scanning a Zillow list or an AI answer need a reason to pick this name. Javier has one - bilingual, Austin-raised, first-time-buyer specialist - and the current site throws it away.

  • The bilingual angle is a wedge into a one-third slice of the market most competitors cannot serve at full-transaction depth.
  • Near-term prize is not "rank #1 for Austin realtor." It is: become the obvious, trusted, bilingual first-time-buyer choice for buyers already searching - and capture them first.

Current-State Audit

Javier has real assets - they are just arranged to compete on the wrong axis. The six core vulnerabilities, with specifics:

What is working (defend these)

  • Real site with real tools. Home valuation + IDX search + neighborhoods - more useful than most agent sites at his stage.
  • Authentic first-time-buyer proof. All three testimonials (Debanhi S., Jinky R., Karina) are first-time buyers who felt guided - a clear, believable niche.
  • Genuine origin story. Jalisco to Chicago to Austin, 12-plus years local, Texas State advertising background - trust equity most agents cannot manufacture.

Vulnerability 1 · The bilingual advantage is buried

  • "Habla Espanol" reads as a small note, not the spine of the brand.
  • In a ~33% Hispanic metro, running the entire transaction in Spanish is the strongest, rarest trust signal Javier owns.
  • The headline ("Austin Real Estate Expert") discards it for the most crowded phrase in the market.

Fix: lead the hero, trust band, and a dedicated Spanish section with the bilingual, Austin-raised story. Done in the demo: hero "Your Austin agent, in your language, on your side," a bilingual feature section, a Spanish lead magnet, and a Spanish FAQ answer.

Vulnerability 2 · Generic headline, generic positioning

  • "Austin Real Estate Expert" is claimable by all ~17,000-plus metro agents.
  • Nothing tells a buyer why Javier over the next Zillow name - no packaged niche, no named program, no "who I am for."

Fix: an ownable position (bilingual, first-time-buyer specialist) plus a named process. Done in the demo: the "First Home, Done Right" program and a four-step "How it works" from strategy call to keys.

Vulnerability 3 · Proof is thin and self-published

  • ~Six portfolio properties, three testimonials, all self-hosted.
  • No aggregate track-record framing, no visible Google/Zillow rating, no "homes closed / families served" counter.
  • Buyers scan for proof in seconds - there is little to scan.

Fix: a proof spine - track-record band (years local, cities covered, families served), real reviews surfaced, animated stat counters. Done in the demo: count-up trust band and credential bar; "families guided" and "client rating" tiles are placeholder-flagged pending Javier's real numbers.

Vulnerability 4 · Unbranded email undercuts a paid domain

  • Public contact email is a personal Gmail, though Javier owns workwithjavi.com.
  • A javier@workwithjavi.com address is a free, five-minute credibility upgrade - a real trust signal on every reply, form receipt, and nurture email.

Fix: stand up a branded domain email; route all capture and nurture through it.

Vulnerability 5 · The vlog is switched off

  • A YouTube channel and on-site Vlog page exist, but the page reads dormant - no current embedded content turning views into leads.
  • Video is the highest-trust format in real estate, sitting idle instead of feeding the funnel.

Fix: reactivate on a lightweight, repeatable cadence (neighborhood tours, first-time-buyer explainers, a Spanish series) embedded on-site as social proof and top-of-funnel. See the 90-Day Plan, Month 3.

Vulnerability 6 · Lead capture is passive

  • Forms and a valuation tool exist, but no lead magnet, no instant response, no visible nurture.
  • A not-ready browser leaves with nothing and is never followed up - the single most expensive leak in a market that starts online and closes with an agent.

Fix: an active capture layer - a free "Austin First-Time Buyer's Roadmap" (English and Spanish), name + email capture, automated nurture into a booked showing. Done in the demo: a styled lead-magnet section with language toggle and demo capture form.

The Lead-Gen Funnel

Magnet, Nurture, Booked showing.

  1. Magnet. A buyer lands on the site or a suburb page and downloads the free "Austin First-Time Buyer's Roadmap" (English or Spanish) for name + email. Anonymous browsers become known contacts.
  2. Instant delivery + welcome. PDF delivered instantly from the branded domain email, with a warm welcome positioning Javier as the trusted bilingual guide.
  3. Nurture sequence. A short automated series in the buyer's language: what to expect, down-payment and program myths, city fit, and social proof (testimonials, vlog clips) - each ending with a soft invite to book a strategy call.
  4. Booked showing. Every CTA points to one low-friction action: a free, no-pressure strategy call - the top of Javier's real four-step process (strategy call, tours, offer/negotiation, keys).
  5. Reputation loop. After closing, an automated review request feeds the proof spine, lifting search visibility and warming the next cohort.

The result is a system, not a hope: every online-first browser is captured, nurtured in their language, and routed to a booked conversation with Javier.

Risk Reversal

  • Foundation work is fixed-scope, fixed-fee, guaranteed delivery. You know exactly what you get and what it costs before anything starts.
  • Performance-based framing on the growth layer. The bilingual-funnel and lead-gen work can be structured so that a portion is tied to agreed outcomes (captured leads, booked calls) rather than paid flat up front, so incentives are aligned and risk is shared.
  • No lock-in on the brand. The espresso + terracotta identity, the copy, and the assets are Javier's. Everything is built on his own domain and email, under his control.

Why Now

  1. The bilingual lane is open and un-owned. No dominant Austin personal-brand agent is credibly positioned as the full-transaction bilingual choice for the one-third Hispanic market. Whoever claims it first keeps it.
  2. Search is shifting to AI answers. Structured, credential-rich, specific sites get named in AI answers; generic "Austin expert" pages do not. Building the bilingual, first-time-buyer content now compounds for years.
  3. Javier already paid for the foundation. Site, tools, testimonials, vlog are sunk cost. Sharpening positioning and adding capture turns idle assets into a system - high-leverage.
  4. The market rewards the specific. In a ~17,000-agent metro with 100% of buyers online and ~88% closing through an agent, the differentiated, capture-ready agent wins the online-first stage. A winnable fight, on the right terms.
